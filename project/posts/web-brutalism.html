The Return of Web Brutalism

Published: October 15, 2025 Category: Design By: Webmaster

Today I'm writing about the fascinating revival of Web Brutalism and how 90s web design is making a comeback in modern web development.

What is Web Brutalism?

Web Brutalism takes inspiration from the architectural movement of the same name. It embraces raw, unpolished aesthetics and focuses on functionality over form. The term "brutalism" comes from the French phrase "béton brut," which means "raw concrete."

In web design, brutalism manifests as:

  • Harsh, contrasting colors
  • Lack of symmetry and balance
  • Monospaced fonts and basic typography
  • Raw HTML elements with minimal styling
  • Visible table borders and form elements
  • An intentionally "undesigned" appearance

The 90s Web Aesthetic

The early web of the 1990s was characterized by limitations in both technology and design knowledge. Most websites were created by amateurs who were learning HTML as they built. This resulted in a distinctive aesthetic that included:

  • Bright background colors
  • Animated GIFs
  • Under construction banners
  • Visitor counters
  • Guestbooks
  • Tiled background images
  • Blinking text and marquees

These elements, once considered outdated, are now being embraced for their nostalgic value and as a reaction against the homogenization of modern web design.

Why is 90s Web Design Making a Comeback?

Several factors contribute to the revival of 90s web aesthetics:

1. Nostalgia

Many web designers and users who grew up during the early days of the internet have a fondness for the aesthetic of that era. This nostalgia is driving a resurgence in designs that evoke the feeling of the early web.

2. Reaction Against Minimalism

After years of flat design and minimalism dominated by white space and subtle colors, designers are rebelling by embracing the opposite: chaotic layouts, bright colors, and decorative elements.

3. Desire for Authenticity

In an era where most websites look similar due to the widespread use of templates and design systems, brutalist and 90s-inspired designs stand out for their uniqueness and individuality.

4. The Rise of Personal Websites

As social media platforms become increasingly commercialized, there's a renewed interest in personal websites and blogs where individuals have complete control over their online presence.

Examples of Modern 90s-Inspired Websites

Here are some notable examples of websites embracing the 90s aesthetic in a modern context:

  • Poolside FM - A music player with a retro Mac OS interface
  • Hypnospace Outlaw - A game that simulates a fictional 90s internet
  • Neocities - A modern take on Geocities that hosts personal websites
  • Windows93.net - An art project that reimagines Windows 95

How to Incorporate 90s Elements Tastefully

If you're inspired to add some 90s flair to your own website, here are some tips:

  • Use bright colors strategically rather than overwhelming the entire design
  • Add one or two animated elements instead of making everything move
  • Choose a retro typography but ensure it remains readable
  • Include nostalgic elements like guestbooks as a fun interactive feature
  • Maintain basic usability principles despite the retro aesthetic

Conclusion

The return of 90s web design represents more than just nostalgia—it's a statement against the uniformity of modern web design. By embracing the raw, unpolished aesthetics of the early web, designers are rediscovering the joy and creativity that characterized the internet's formative years.
